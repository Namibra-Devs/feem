Building Self Esteem

Self-esteem is a having a strong level of confidence and belief in oneself. It is often referred to as wielding a high sense of personal belief and the inner conviction in your abilities including your physical outlook. According to the Queensland Government (2024), having a healthy self-esteem is when you generally feel good about yourself and your accomplishments as well as feeling confident to try new things and challenge yourself. Having a low self-esteem means that you do not believe that you are capable of anything and that you do not deserve any form of special treatment. People with low self-esteem often have the tendency to disbelief in their abilities and are often bullied and maltreated by people who recognise their weaknesses. A low self-esteem can be very detrimental to many young people especially girls as it places them in a vulnerable state of yearning to be recognised by a particular group of people.

For example, girls who belief that they are not physically attractive are often preys for predators who abuse them by making them think that no other individual will ever want them except they the predators. Girls caught up in the web of low self-esteem often conclude that their lives are dependent on their abusers hence have no where to go. Girls of that nature equally belief that nothing good could ever happen in their lives and they often fear to take initiatives or aspire for greatness. The organisation as part of its mandates, instils in young women a high-level self-esteem to believe in themselves, appreciate who they are and be filled with the conviction that they can achieve a lot of things. We have been doing this in some schools, and we hope to continue to hammer this message of self-belief. These moves we belief will deliver many young women out of toxic relations and help to protect them from coercive sexual relations.
