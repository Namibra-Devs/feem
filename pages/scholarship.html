Educational Support

According to the United Nations (UNICEF), there are more than 1.1 billion girls in the world today. There are 31 million girls of primary school age out of school. Of these, 17 million are expected to never enter school according to UNESCO. Also, UNESCO estimates that there are 34 million female adolescents out of school while almost a quaternary of young women ages 15-24 today numbering 116 million in developing countries who have never completed primary school and so lack the skills for work and two thirds of the 774 million illiterate people in the world are said to be females. The World Bank indicates that, in Ghana, the Gender Parity Index (GPI) for Gross Enrolment in tertiary education in Ghana stood at 0.8471 percent in 2019, which is an indication that learning opportunities favoured more men than women (Sharma et al., 2021). UNICEF estimates that, if all women had secondary education there would be 49 percent fewer child deaths. Again, if all women had secondary education in Sub-Saharan Africa, South and West Asia, child marriages would have dropped to 64 percent. A powerful agent of change is an educated woman. Educate a woman you are sure that society will become better than it is already.

Women and girls are the drivers of Sustainable development. Healthy educated and empowered women and girls will foster the change needed to create empowered communities and consequently a sustainable and peaceful future for all, as envisioned by the 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development. Evidence shows that, when investment is made on women and girls, there is the creation of a ripple effect that yields multiple benefits, not only to the women and girls but also to families, communities and the country at large. Girls’ education, particularly at the secondary level, is the “magic bullet” for development and the most consistent driver of development goals. Girls’ education is one of the most powerful investments for development. Investing in girls can accelerate the fight against poverty and empower them to be valuable contributors to their communities and society at large.

Educated young women are less likely to give birth in childhood, die during childbirth, more likely to send their children to school, and better able to protect themselves and their children from malnutrition, HIV/AIDS, trafficking, and sexual exploitation. An educated girl is more likely to get a job and earn a higher wage, and her nation’s economy is likely to benefit as a result and more, so an educated girl is more likely to enrol her children into school and can train and discipline them in the right manner. Education is one major form of empowering girls as through that they can understand a whole number of issues and are able to take decisions on their own. The initiative capitalizes on empowering young girls on education by encouraging them to reach higher levels in the educational ladder. The aim of the Organization is to ensure that some girls at the Junior High School and Senior High School levels in Ghana who are burdened financially be supported with some basic needs for their survival.

More specifically, it is the mandate of the organization to support some of the girls at the Junior High School level with food and other basic needs to help them concentrate in school and pass their exam to enable them to move smoothly in their educational ladder. They will also be guided towards making the right career paths and moving towards the right direction which is going to lead them to greater heights. It must be stated that indeed some girls do go through a lot of struggles as the school feeding system by the Government is not extended to the Junior High School level. The Initiative therefore has it as part of its plans to take care of the feeding and basic needs of these girls to help them focus more on their academics. The aim at the end of the day is that these girls will be guided in the right way to help them develop mindsets that are all about moving and accelerating to the highest levels in their lives. As part of this agenda of supporting young women’s education is our scholarship scheme for young women in Ghana. The scholarship package includes the payment of fees, a semester stipend and a laptop. The first scholarship recipient is Christabel Ampomah, a daughter of a single mother. Christabel is first among her siblings making her way on the higher educational ladder and we believe she will be an agent of positive transformational change in her family and community.
