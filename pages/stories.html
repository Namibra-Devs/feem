Impact Stories

January 7th, 2023.

In Feo in the Bongo District of the Upper East Region of Ghana.

In 7th January 2023 the Female Empowerment (FEEM) Initiative took it upon itself to fulfill a part of its missions by educating the young women (The UNFPA Young and Wise team) in the Feo Community in the Bongo District of the Upper East Region of Ghana on contraceptive methods as well providing them a means of livelihood thus liquid soap making. It was a two-pronged approach of empowerment. The first activity included giving them the knowledge on the types of contraceptive methods. Some of the girls acknowledged that they were hearing and seeing the types of contraceptive methods for the first time. At the end of the session on contraceptive methods, they all admitted that they had learnt something new and were interested in some of the methods of contraception. The second activity was on the skill training on liquid soap making. All girls developed the practical skill of liquid soap making. The team at FEEM urged them to sell the liquid soap produced on that day for the proceeds to be used for another production. In addition to that FEEM added the young women with a seed capital of 300.00 for them to add for the production of the liquid soap to be sold for some proceeds and profits.

The profits are to be saved and used to support each other especially if some of them needed sanitary pads or urgently need to pay school fees or need assistance on any other important thing in future. The activities for the day ended well with majority of them expressing their joy and gratitude for the new knowledge they had gained and their readiness to put into practice everything learnt. The training on the liquid soap skill training led to the young women making their own production of the liquid soap.

The ladies used the start-up to produce three basins of the soap of which they sold and made some profits out of the sale they made. The profits made were further on used to produce six basins of the liquid soap of which it has been sold to make more profits.

"The journey was challenging, but the support made all the difference." the women had successfully produced and sold liquid soap, generating profits that enabled them to continue producing more. The initiative left them with newfound skills, financial independence, and a sense of empowerment, ready to apply their knowledge and support one another in the future. This event was a testament to the power of education and practical training in driving sustainable change in their lives.

- @ Bongo
